About Us

Learn more about the Black Student Union at EMCC

Mission

Black Student Union is a collective created to provide a safe space for African American students' progression at EMCC. We discuss social, political, and economic standpoints in our community.

Purpose

  • To address problems relating to educational, economic, social and political conditions of African American on campus and in the community
  • To promote better understanding between African Americans and non African Americans on campus and in the community
  • To hold fundraising projects to finance any endeavors the organization feels necessary

Club Logo

Inspired by the Sankofa symbol from the Akan people of West Africa, Sankofa is translated to "it is not taboo to go back and fetch what you forgot". Sankofa teaches us that we must go back to our roots in order to move forward - you can't go forward unless you understand your history.

The Black Student Union at EMCC seeks to honor our past, and transform our future.

Interested in running for a Club Officer position? Officer Elections for the Fall 2025 year will occur soon between September-October. Contact bsu@estrellamountain.edu for more information. Open Positions include:

  • President- Serve as the official representative of the BSU and conduct business previously approved by the Executive Board and Club Advisors; Facilitate all general body and executive board meetings with effective leadership
  • Vice President- Assists President; Coordinates all committees and oversees the annual Welcome Week/Black Student Welcome Activities
  • Secretary- Maintain accurate and permanent record of general club, executive and special meeting minutes
  • Treasurer- Oversees Club Fundraising, Club Budget, and is responsible for maintaining records for accounts
  • Historian- Creates and Maintains pictures and archives for BSU; Oversees content for Club Website and Social Media
  • Programming Director- Lead and provide oversight to all Black Student Union club activities and events; Oversees and tracks Club Participation Points and co-coordinates the Black History and Achievement Month (BHAM) events.
